Business Name: G&D Kézműves Cukrászda és Pékség (Szó ami Szó)

Location: Balatonkenese, Hungary

Type: Family-owned artisan pastry shop and bakery

Founders:
- Szó Katalin (mother, originally a psychologist)
- Szó Gellért (pastry chef)
- Szó Dániel (baker)

Brand Origin:
The name “G&D” comes from the initials of the two brothers, Gellért and Dániel. The phrase “Szó ami Szó” reflects honesty, authenticity, and standing by quality.

Core Identity:
A deeply artisanal, family-driven pâtisserie and bakery combining French technique with Hungarian tradition. Everything is made with precision, care, and high-quality ingredients, often including French butter and flour and long fermentation processes.

Awards & Recognition:
- Winner of “Ország Tortája” (Hungary’s Cake of the Year) multiple times (2015, 2016)
- Awarded best croissant in Hungary (2017)
- Named “Best Bakery of the Year” (Street Kitchen Guide, 2025)
- Widely regarded as one of the best bakeries in Hungary, often compared to top European pâtisseries

Products:
- Artisan sourdough breads (long fermentation, high craftsmanship)
- Croissants (considered among the best in the country)
- French-style pastries and viennoiserie
- Award-winning cakes
- Seasonal and locally inspired creations

Philosophy:
Quality above everything. No compromise on ingredients, technique, or time. A fusion of precision and emotion: French baking discipline combined with Hungarian soul and family warmth.

Atmosphere:
A garden-centered, welcoming bakery near Lake Balaton, where guests can sit under trees and experience slow, high-quality mornings. Strong local loyalty combined with destination-level appeal.

Customer Perception:
Locals and returning guests see it as a hidden gem and a ritual destination. Many consider it world-class, comparable to top bakeries in France or Belgium.

Important Emotional Angles:
- Family story (mother + two sons)
- Craftsmanship and discipline
- Awards as proof of excellence
- A “destination bakery” feeling
- Personal relationships with returning guests
- Understated excellence (not flashy, but exceptional)
